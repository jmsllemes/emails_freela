Dear doctor,

Do you have patients with autoimmune pulmonary alveolar proteinosis (aPAP) or patients exhibiting symptoms of aPAP who want to learn more about the condition?

We have launched a new website just for them. Share the link with your patients so they can learn more about the disease and find the resources they need: https://www.apapclearpath.com/patient/

You may have more aPAP patients than you think1-3

aPAP presents with symptoms common to other pulmonary illnesses, making it difficult to diagnose.2,4 In the time it takes for someone to be accurately diagnosed with aPAP, their quality of life may be negatively impacted.2,5,6

A simple, accurate, no-cost, and noninvasive blood test can help you obtain a definitive diagnosis sooner.2,7

When you see:

Common chronic pulmonary symptoms2,6,8

+

An abnormal computerized tomography scan2,6,8

+

Other pulmonary infection diagnoses that are refractory to treatment2,6,8

Then, it is time to test for granulocyte-macrophage colony-stimulating factor (GM-CSF) autoantibodies.2,9
